Zeyun(Hughes) Zhao

I am a PhD student in Biomedical Engineering at Vanderbilt University, specializing in Medical AI, under the supervision of Dr. Ruogu Fang. Before joining Vanderbilt, I pursued my PhD training in Biomedical Engineering at the University of Florida. My research focuses on artificial intelligence for medical imaging, neuroimaging, and precision medicine, with an expected graduation in 2029. I earned my BS in Computer Science and Technology, with a minor in Finance, from Jiangnan University in China, and my MS in Artificial Intelligence from Xiamen University, where I was supervised by Dr. Shaozi Li and Dr. Zhiming Luo.

My interests in BME center around using advanced AI technologies to solve medical problems, especially in brain health. My research mainly focuses on Medical Image Analysis, Large Language Models, Vision-Language Models, Foundation Models, and Agentic AI. I am passionate about identifying and solving valuable challenges in this field. Additionally, I strive to be a leader and entrepreneur in my community and to use my passion for science to create positive change in the world.

Outside of work, I enjoy recharging outdoors and exploring new recipes. I like hiking, scuba diving, skiing, and photography.

I am very interested in how advanced AI techniques can solve medical problems and how lab findings can be translated to the commercial market. Please feel free to contact me if you are interested in my research.
